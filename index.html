About me

I am a Masters student in Game Design and Production at Aalto university. This is a collection of my games, game projects among other miscellaneous projects.

Battle Beetles

Game Description


An 2nd person camera mulitplayer game


THE GAME

GAME IDEATION

Players set out to compete against each other in a multiplayer setting, avoiding environmental hazards and above all, avoid getting knocked off their beetle. If knocked off their beetle, players become extremely vulnerable and open to elimination in this Beetle Royale!

Game Map

GAME DESIGN

The game set out to experiment with a 2nd person camera, something not typically used in video games. Players riding beetles served as an excellent method for testing this, as the beetle could hold the camera, yet the player actually controlled the rider.

Where The Birds Are

Game Description


A calm, contemplative music-based game.

The theme was “the feeling associated with your home” - Since we are in Finland, the natural choice was a forest-based game.
Our game was also one of the 6 games chosen from the 260 made in 2019 to receive the Finnish Game Jam award.



THE GAME

GAME IDEATION

Our team intended to create a wonderful audio ambient environment that grows in complexity and beauty as the player continues to progress. The visuals were meant to be low-poly complemented by nice lighting and fog effects to give the sense of a deep forest.

Something very important for our game is the synchronization of sounds: the sounds are triggered at the exact moment the birdhouse is placed, and mixes seamlessly with the existing music in the game.

Stage 1

GAME DESIGN

The game conists of three stages, intially giving the player a handful of trees upon which to place birdhouses.

The birdhouses are divided in two colors, the player can see and hear a connection form if two birdhouses of the same color are placed on the same height level facing each other.

Upon placement, the ambient music is expanded upon from 24 sounds, all created during the jam. As the number of birdhouses increases, so does the complexity of the music. As the camera pans out, the player also begins to see other NPCs, all forming a community, with the implication that this is home for not only the birds, but also the players.

Stage 2

THE TEAM

We were a team of 5, consisting of two programmers, a sound designer, a 2d artist, and myself.

Since we had a few programmers, my role consisted of mostly 3d artwork, creating textures assets in the game, the background of the scene, level design, and creating 3d models as well as modelling the rocks, leaves and birds.
I also did some programming jobs, including bird behavior.

Built in Unity.

Agents Light

Game Description


The game consisted of navigating your character, a spy, through an enemy building in an attempt to rescue a fellow spy.

The theme for the jam was “only one”, so, we decided that the player would have a very limited field of vision in the form of an orb. They may throw this orb a number of times to be able to see enemy activity in the area.


The project can be found on Github


THE GAME

GAME IDEATION

The theme was a large driving factor in our game design.

We loved the idea of limiting a player in a constructive way that is both challenging and enjoyable.

This led us to the idea of limiting their sight to *one* area at a time, restricting their abilities to navigate.

GAME DESIGN

The game features a multicolored map, when the player passes by, it lights up and remains lit, serving as a guide to where they have already explored.

The player must then try to form an outline of the area while avoiding the guards to find the pathway to their captured colleague.

Built in Unity.

Escape Pod VR

Game Description


The theme was “Sensory deprivation”. The game takes place on a stranded escape pod, the player starts out with limited senses and has to solve puzzles using those senses to regain functionality in the pod as well as their senses.


The project can be downloaded Here


THE GAME

GAME IDEATION

The inspiration for this game was a fun limitation of the players sensing capabilities, and try to see how it influences puzzle-solving behavior.

This led me to XR, because it seemed like the ideal way to isolate players and therefore control what senses they have and which they do not.

Trying to get home

GAME DESIGN

Players initially start with touch only, having to feel the walls for the light switch.

This enables emergency lighting, granting sight and sound.
Then, they must solve a navigation puzzle, since the sound has turned back on.

Finally, they must combine several of the senses to solve the final puzzle and get the ship working.
Built in Unity.

Orbs need to be calibrated

THE TEAM

For this game, there were only two team members: Atte Hanski and myself. Since I was the only one with experience developing in VR, I mainly build the game and the scene and helped with programming. Atte wrote a majority of the code and helped with the asset creation.
