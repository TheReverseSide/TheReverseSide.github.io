Who I am

I am a researcher in the Computer Science department at the University of Helsinki, Finland, conducting research in the realm of Human-Computer Interaction, drawing upon psychology, cognitive science, and Human-Centered design usability principles. optimizing user experience via user research and user-centred design My background is in psychology and cognitive science

My interests and motivation

I have a passion to apply that knowledge to games, particularly serious games. I am inspired by Psyon games and how they make games based on science and education fun and accessible" I loved their Antidote game (and who doesnt love Trump vs Science and TrumpGO? - add links to all of this on website) Another source of inspiration is due to my background in Eastern philosophy and meditation. As a long-time meditator and an educator, the potential for video games to mass distribute methods for people to become more grateful, contemplative and content drives me to expand and innovate in this field. My previous experience in physiological equipment will be useful when neurogaming and related fields become increasingly common (Adam Gazzaley)




Philosophy and background

Why I make games

Cognitive Science and Psychology

Knowing the brain, how it works and where the weak spots are allows you to place challenges exactly where you want to challenge players in specific ways.  Cognitive science, being the study of perception, memory, attention, learning, reasoning and problem solving, is extremely relevant because all of those neurological processes are involved as players are playing the games.  Studying psychology helped me develop an intuitive understanding of core human needs, learn how to experiment creatively, and to make decisions in the face of uncertainty.

Education

Further, as education moves more and more towards digitalization (and in some cases gamification), a deep understanding of how the brain interacts with digital media is becoming more and more important.

NeuroGame TD

TitleTBD: NeuroGame?

An ongoing project integrating a tower-defense and quiz games, teaching the cognitive sciences.

Game Details

This game is an attempt to branch the fields of video games and psychology/cognitive science by providing short lessons on these topics and their role in video games.

For this project I wanted to create some way to help people learn about some of my favorite fields: psychology and cognitive science. Specifically, I want to help shed light on how exactly these fields are used in one of my favorite hobbies: video games. I hope to inspire in the gamer an interest in the study of human behavior and cognition that they may not have had before. and thought that TowerDefense is a game design for an education game since it is based on rounds/waves, those could be separately by the gradual leveling of the player + change in game difficulty, and the rewards for doing well in the game could easily be implemented as something that every TD player needs: More towers!

NeuroGame's GitHub Page

Tower Defense Layout

I chose tower defense because it is naturally round based, and provided a good framework upon which I could place quiz rounds. Further, the continual accumulation of resources in TD games allowed me to easily provide rewards for correctly answering questions.

Illustrations

As can be seen below, I designed and implemented some images and sprites for the game: particularly, locks, brain images, in-game currency, and a background. These are freely available on ADD SITES, which can be found via the menu.

Psychology and Cognitive Science

I think people have the habit of thinking of psychology as not very applicable to the real world, something that thrives in the lab but does nto meet expectations in the real world, through this game I wanted to show that this is not the case. Mention why learning about this is important in the context of game design
